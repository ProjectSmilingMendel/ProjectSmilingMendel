Design of the Experiment and expected expenditure:


Phase-1: Proof of concept (Funds Required : 376,000 INR)
Identification of the level of overexpression of the genes of interest required:

  1. Performing a PCR of the desired genes and cloning them into suitable vectors
  2. Synthesis of siderophores for getting the proteins to be used in spot assays and toxicity analysis.
  3. Determination of the minimum concentration of siderophore required to starve the fungal pathogens to death
  4. Toxicity analysis to get the upper limit for siderophore concentration beyond which plants may display some symptoms of toxicity.
  5. Correlating the levels of siderophore concentration required with the level of over-expression of the desired genes



Phase-2: Development of the transformed bacteria (Funds required 415,450 INR)
  1. Genome integration:
    1. Without duplication of the surrounding chromosomal areas, the 4363 bp Escherichia coli plasmid pBR322 sequence has been integrated at the met, pro, or leuB locus of the Bacillus subtilis chromosome.
    2. It has been tested that a 17 kb DNA segment cloned in pBR322 could be easily transformed into the B. subtilis chromosome. Using chromosomal DNA extracted from a strain that already possessed a pBR322 sequence at a different locus, the sequential transformation was used to successfully insert a second pBR322 sequence at various locations on the chromosome.
    3. A third pBR322 sequence might be added similarly. This technique allows two or three pBR322 genes to be inserted at unrelated loci without changing the B. subtilis genome's general structure.
  2. Isolation and purification of the transformed bacterial colonies
    1. We shall be cloning the desired genes into pBR322 plasmid and then integrate them into the genome of B.subtilis.
    2. pBR322 has tetracycline resistance genes and ampicillin resistance genes which can be used as selectable markers for isolating the transformed bacterial colonies
    3. Further it shall be verified that the isolated bacterial colonies are producing the required proteins by secretome analysis of the pure culture
  3. Secretome analysis to identify any novel and/or unwanted protein-protein interactions: We shall be performing Affinity-Purification Mass Spectrometry for this purpose.
