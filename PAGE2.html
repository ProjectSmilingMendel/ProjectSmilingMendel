Our current Proposal:
Our current proposal is to manipulate the genome of Bacillus subtilis (strain: CU741 along with others) by inserting multiple copies of the genes: irp2, iroN, iucA from the genome of E.coli . These genes encode proteins called hydroxamate siderophores
Mechanism of action of hydroxamate siderophores: Siderophores are iron chelating compounds produced by a variety of organisms , and they generally have ferric chelates with stability constants of approximately 1030. Plants produce siderophores when they have a deficiency of iron and it has been shown that hydroxamate siderophores have a high affinity to iron in the pH range from 4 to 10 and are more effective at treating iron chlorosis than FeCl3.
Escherichia coli is a bacteria found in the lower intestines of warm blooded animals. It is known to produce various virulence factors including aerobactin, yersiniabactin and salmochelin. These three virulence factors, in particular happen to be hydroxamate siderophores which are used for chelating iron from the intestines and the urinary tract. They are even able to displace iron from some other siderophores.
Aerobactin is encoded by iucDBAC gene cluster , salmochelin is encoded by iroA gene cluster and yersiniabactin is encoded by HPI and irp2, irpl, ybtSETUXPQA gene clusters.
These three siderophores( aerobactin , yersiniabactin and salmochelin), being present in the intestines of animals, is speculated not to be bioavailable to most soil fungi, but at the same time is bioavailable to plants. This hypothesis can be tested by culturing soil fungal pathogens and plant seedlings separately with the iron-chelated form of these siderophores as the only iron source.
Because of the fact that these siderophores have the capability to displace iron from other siderophores, given enough time, given a mixture containing these siderophores and others, the mole fraction of these siderophores in iron chelated form will be greater than that of other siderophores. If these iron chelated compounds can be absorbed by the plants but not by soil fungi, they may cause iron deficiency in fungi and can also cure iron deficiency in plants. This gives them a potential fungicidal action as well as a mineral nutrition function.
Bacillus subtilis is a soil dwelling bacteria that is known to have antifungal action and is generally harmless to plants.
Insertion aerobactin gene(iucA), yersiniabactin gene (irp2), salmochelin gene (iroN) into Bacillus subtilis should cause the bacillus to produce these siderophores in the soil, adding to the antifungal action of B.subtilis.
Another experiment to verify effectiveness of these siderophores would be to culture the fungi in separate cultures of transformed bacillus and compare it against a culture of fungi in cultures containing reference non-transformed bacillus.
